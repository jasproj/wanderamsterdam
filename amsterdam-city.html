About Amsterdam

Amsterdam is the smallest of the major European capitals — most of the central city is walkable in 30 minutes. The 17th-century canal ring (UNESCO World Heritage) is the structural feature; the four main canals (Singel, Herengracht, Keizersgracht, Prinsengracht) form concentric semicircles around the medieval center. Most tours we work with use the canals as their backbone: 1-hour open-boat cruises from Central Station, 90-minute private tours that include drinks, evening dinner cruises, and bike tours that follow the canal banks.

If you have one day, the standard high-value pattern is a 1-hour canal cruise in the morning + a 2-hour walking tour of the Jordaan (the working-class-turned-trendy neighborhood west of the canals) + an afternoon at either the Rijksmuseum or Van Gogh Museum. Combined museum + canal tickets save 15-20% over individual entries. Bike rental and a guided bike tour are the most-locally-respected ways to see the city; standard rental rates run €15-20/day.

What to know before you book

  • Central canal ring is UNESCO World Heritage — most tours use the canals as the spine
  • Rijksmuseum + Van Gogh Museum combo tickets save 15-20% over individual entries
  • Bike rental is the most-locally-respected transport — €15-20/day standard rate
  • Best months: April-September; June-August can be crowded with cruise traffic
  • Open-boat canal cruises (1 hr) depart from Central Station every 15-30 min
  • Walking tours of the Jordaan are the strongest non-canal pattern
